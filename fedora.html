Fedora

Dad's Choice

If you choose Fedora, you'll be in good company. Linus Torvalds, the creator of the Linux kernel himself uses it, and the creator of this website does too! Great minds think alike, right?

Why Choose Fedora?

Choose fedora if you like(relatively) vanilla software, delivered the way the developers intended. Fedora is also a good choice if you think rolling releases like Arch are too unstable, but find that Ubuntu or Debian update too slowly.

Fedora offers a unique combination of continuous updates for most software, but it reserves major version changes, and fundamental system changes for new, major releases.

Finally, they are often the first to market to test new pieces of software, such as Wayland or Pipewire. They are also the only distro on this site that offers an Atomic version.

Reasons to Avoid

None :-)

More seriously though, fedora does have a couple of reasons why some people might want to avoid it

  • You don't like testing new software
  • You hate Red Hat/IBM
  • You want an even Faster update cadence

Consider testing releases in a VM before switching.
